# Film Brief: 罪与罚

## Logline

In a feverish Petersburg of yellow rooms, pawned objects, police questions, and wet streets, a young former student commits murder to prove he is above ordinary law, then discovers that conscience is more exacting than any court.

## Source Boundary

- Source: Fyodor Dostoevsky, *Crime and Punishment* (1866), public domain.
- This project writes original Chinese narration and original prompts.
- Do not copy wording from modern translations.
- Use the novel's public-domain characters and dramatic situation, but compress events into a short film prototype rather than a full plot summary.

## Format

- Prototype length: 2-4 minutes for the first pipeline run.
- Target future form: feature-film style psychological literary drama.
- Voice: restrained Chinese narration, moral pressure rather than explanation.
- Visual language: 1860s Saint Petersburg, cramped rented rooms, stairwells, pawnshop interiors, police office, canal fog, candlelight, sickly summer heat, snow-muted Siberian epilogue.

## Story Spine

1. Petersburg presses down on Raskolnikov before he acts.
2. He leaves his narrow room carrying a hidden axe.
3. The pawnbroker's apartment becomes a trap of doors, keys, and breath.
4. Violence happens off the center of the frame; the room remembers it.
5. He hides the stolen objects and finds no freedom in them.
6. Fever turns every street and stairwell into accusation.
7. Porfiry questions him with courtesy sharp enough to wound.
8. Sonya appears as witness, mercy, and mirror.
9. The confession begins before he speaks it aloud.
10. The city hears his guilt in the public square.
11. Siberia strips the theory from him and leaves only endurance.
12. A small dawn suggests punishment may become responsibility, not erasure.

## Production Goal

The first run is not trying to create a finished feature film. It is a proving ground for Narrascape's core promise: script -> AI director -> reference-aware generation -> generated video -> film timeline -> QA -> rework -> final assembly.

## Emotional Arc

- Beginning: intellectual arrogance under poverty and heat.
- Middle: panic, disgust, and self-division.
- Turn: Sonya and Porfiry make denial harder than confession.
- Ending: punishment remains real, but moral awakening becomes visible.

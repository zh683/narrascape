# Film Brief: 罪与罚

## Logline

一个贫穷、骄傲、发热的前大学生试图用一场谋杀证明自己高于普通道德，却在彼得堡潮湿的街道、审讯室的礼貌沉默、索尼娅的凝视和西伯利亚的清晨里，发现良心比任何理论都更难逃脱。

## Source Boundary

- Source: Fyodor Dostoevsky, *Crime and Punishment* (1866), public domain.
- This project uses original Chinese narration and original AI generation prompts.
- Do not copy wording from modern translations.
- Keep the novel's public-domain characters and dramatic situation, but build a short cinematic proof-of-work rather than a plot summary.

## Rebuild Goal

This rebuild is a production-minded first pass, not a salvage of the failed run. The objective is to test whether Narrascape can preserve literary intent through:

1. script spine
2. AI director pre-production
3. locked character and scene references
4. storyboard-bound shot contracts
5. generated image and generated video
6. film timeline assembly
7. QA and rework loop

The film must fail loudly in production mode when key director stages are not LLM-driven. Preview mode may use local placeholders only to verify wiring.

## Format

- Prototype length: 2-4 minutes.
- Shot count: 12.
- Language: Chinese narration.
- Tone: restrained psychological drama, not crime spectacle.
- Visual world: 1860s Saint Petersburg, cramped yellow rooms, stairs, pawnshop interiors, canal fog, official paper, candlelight, rain-dark stone, Siberian dawn.

## Dramatic Spine

1. The city presses down before the crime.
2. Raskolnikov prepares his theory as if it were courage.
3. The pawnbroker flat becomes doors, keys, breath, and bad light.
4. Violence is implied by aftermath, never glamorized.
5. Stolen objects prove useless.
6. Fever turns every face into accusation.
7. Porfiry turns politeness into pressure.
8. Sonya becomes the first room where truth can remain alive.
9. Confession starts inside the body before words.
10. The public square makes guilt visible.
11. Siberia removes theatrical self-importance.
12. Dawn offers no erasure, only the beginning of responsibility.

## Visual Doctrine

- Faces should be tired, uneven, and morally exposed.
- Rooms must feel too small for thought.
- Weapons should be concealed or absent from frame unless a shot explicitly requires implication; avoid visible axe imagery.
- Violence is off-center, off-screen, or represented by objects.
- No modern props, cars, electric signage, readable text, subtitles inside generated images, logos, platform watermarks, `AI生成`, `即梦AI`, or app labels.
- Avoid handsome fashion-model Raskolnikov. He is gaunt, sleep-deprived, proud, and physically depleted.

## Production Success Criteria

- Raskolnikov remains the same gaunt young man with the same worn student coat.
- Sonya remains plain, exhausted, and steady; her small cross is visible in key shots.
- Porfiry is a civil magistrate, not a modern detective.
- Locations are distinct: street, rented room, pawnbroker flat, police office, Sonya room, public square, Siberian yard.
- Storyboard frame ids and reference ids appear in director contracts and reference plates.
- Generated-video clips feed `film_timeline.yaml`; image fallback is only a controlled preview fallback.
